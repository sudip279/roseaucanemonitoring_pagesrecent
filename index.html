Vegetation Dieback & Roseau Cane Classification in the Lower MRD
Large-scale historical vegetation and land-change analysis across the lower Mississippi River Delta using Landsat time series (2001–2021). Roseau cane was successfully extracted from spectrally complex coastal wetlands using SVM classification. A 9-year NDVI change analysis (2010–2019) identified ~51 km² of dieback, of which 65.8% occurred within Roseau cane areas — confirming significantly higher vulnerability compared to other marsh vegetation. Mid-scale WorldView-2 classification further distinguished Delta vs. European Phragmites variants at 90.8% overall accuracy (κ = 0.90). Small-scale drone mapping at Pass-A-Loutre validated habitat classification with SVM at over 85% accuracy.

Mapping Phragmites australis Dynamics via U-Net on Time Series Sentinel-2
A U-Net deep learning model (ResNet-34 encoder) applied to fall-season Sentinel-2 composites (2019–2024) for annual pixel-level classification of Roseau cane across the lower MRD. The model achieved 90–98% overall accuracy and κ = 0.85–0.98. Phragmites coverage was tracked annually: declined from 248.2 km² (2019) to 217.5 km² (2021) under hurricane and freeze disturbances, recovered to 235.3 km² (2022), dropped to 214.1 km² (2023) under extreme drought, then rebounded to 237.6 km² (2024) — demonstrating high sensitivity to stress and strong resilience.

Comprehensive multi-scale classification methodology combining Landsat/Sentinel-2 large-area mapping with drone-based fine-scale validation at Pass-A-Loutre. Drone Monitoring Across Four Coastal Regions
UAV surveys across four ecologically distinct Louisiana coastal sites: Lower MRD (Pass-A-Loutre), Rockefeller Wildlife Refuge, Atchafalaya Basin, and Vermilion Bay. REGRESSION · R1
StoryMaps
Assessing Roseau Cane Health via Scale Insect Density & Multi-Sensor NDVI Regression
Regression analysis correlating field-measured Nipponaclerda biwakoensis (scale insect) density with NDVI derived from Landsat (30 m), Sentinel-2 (10 m), PlanetScope (3 m), and drone orthophotos (~8 cm) at 65 sampling plots in the lower MRD. Results confirm that higher scale density corresponds to reduced NDVI (Hypothesis 1 validated). Contrary to expectation, coarser-resolution sensors showed stronger correlation — indicating that mixed pixel effects in very high-resolution imagery require subpixel-level analysis (Hypothesis 2 not validated). Presented at AAG 2025, Detroit.
