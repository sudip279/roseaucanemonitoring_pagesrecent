Overview

This work developed a comprehensive multi-scale classification methodology combining large-area satellite mapping (Landsat, Sentinel-2) with fine-scale drone validation at Pass-A-Loutre. A novel drone sampling strategy was developed specifically to address the limited accessibility challenge in coastal wetland environments — enabling adequate sampling even in areas not reachable by boat.

SVM classification of drone imagery produced reliable habitat maps at over 85% accuracy, with detailed separation of Delta vs. European Phragmites and six additional land cover classes. This method forms the methodological foundation for all subsequent drone monitoring work.

Key Methodological Contributions

Novel drone sampling strategy: A customized stake-mounted target system was developed to stabilize survey control points in soft mud and shallow water — solving the core accessibility limitation in wetland drone mapping.

Multi-scale integration: Drone results directly validated Landsat and WorldView-2 classifications, establishing the consistency and reliability of the broader mapping framework from 30 m down to 8 cm resolution.

Temporal baseline: Initial drone surveys (Fall 2020 to May 2022) at Pass-A-Loutre established the habitat change baseline that underpins the 5-year monitoring work (D3).

Overview

UAV surveys were conducted across four ecologically distinct Louisiana coastal sites to characterize Roseau cane distribution and habitat conditions at fine scale. A deep learning (U-Net) classifier was applied to high-resolution drone orthomosaics at each site, producing 8-class land cover maps. Site-specific challenges (shallow water, sun glint, launch logistics) were addressed through iterative field protocol optimization.

SITE 01
Lower MRD — Pass-A-Loutre
Primary long-term monitoring site. Dense Roseau cane stands dominated by Delta phenotype. Deep-water access via two-boat operation.
SITE 02
Rockefeller Wildlife Refuge
Extremely shallow water levels required airboat support from refuge director. Largest drone coverage area; expanded mapping plan optimized over five test flights.
SITE 03
Atchafalaya Basin
Shallow water and no boat launch for industrial drones. Phantom 4 with natural color camera deployed from boat. Plant types confirmed by angle observation.
SITE 04
Vermilion Bay
Significant open water presence causing sun-glint and rolling artifacts. Flight parameters adapted based on sun angle; strategies refined for fall 2025.

Equipment & Field Methods

Platform: DJI Matrice 600 Pro with MicaSense RedEdge-MX dual camera system (10 spectral bands). Flights conducted at 120 m altitude, 75% overlap, 8 m/s speed. Orthomosaics generated at ~8 cm spatial resolution via Agisoft Metashape photogrammetric processing.

Control targets: Customized stake-mounted targets (24-inch and 36-inch stakes) for stable ground control in soft mud. Larger target dimensions adopted starting Fall 2023 to improve recognition accuracy.

Reflectance calibration: Calibration panel measurements taken immediately before and after each flight to ensure radiometric consistency across dates.

Overview

Since 2022, continuous fine-scale drone monitoring of Roseau cane habitat has been conducted at the Pass-A-Loutre site in the lower Mississippi River Delta (MRD), with surveys each summer and fall season. The goal is mid-term resilience analysis — quantifying habitat changes in response to disturbance events including the 2021 freeze, hurricane impacts, and the extreme drought of 2023. The same DJI Matrice 600 Pro platform with MicaSense RedEdge-MX dual camera system, flight settings, and U-Net deep learning classification (8 land cover classes) are applied consistently across all surveys to ensure full temporal comparability.

Seven classified surveys are now available spanning Fall 2022 through Fall 2025. The October 2024 survey achieved the highest documented accuracy at 92% overall accuracy (κ = 0.89), validated via full confusion matrix analysis.
