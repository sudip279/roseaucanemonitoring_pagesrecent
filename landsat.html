01

Research Overview

Study Motivation & Scope

This research focused on Roseau cane (Phragmites australis) dieback assessment in the lower Mississippi River Delta (MRD) of Louisiana. A comprehensive, systematic multi-source remote sensing method was developed for assessing wetland and Roseau cane dieback and habitat dynamics from three scale levels — large, mid, and small scale.

Large-scale historical vegetation and land-change analyses were conducted across the lower MRD based on Landsat data over the past two decades (2001–2021). A strong increasing trend of vegetation was found since 2005. Around 51 km² of dieback area was detected, accounting for 11% of overall vegetation coverage. Critically, 65.8% of this dieback occurred in the Roseau cane area from 2010 to 2019, even though Roseau cane accounted for only 49.7% of overall vegetation coverage in 2010 — confirming that Roseau cane is significantly more vulnerable to dieback than other wetland vegetation.

02

Vegetation Dieback Assessment (Landsat NDVI Change, 2010–2019)

NDVI difference mapping between 2010 and 2019 was used to classify vegetation dieback levels across the lower MRD. Most dieback and vegetation loss areas were located in the outskirt east and south regions of the lower MRD, indicating these areas should receive priority conservation attention. Relatively stable vegetation areas accounted for 65.68% of coverage.

The analysis also identified 20.98% of areas that changed from non-vegetation to vegetated — largely in the northern MRD due to sediment deposits, marsh protection projects, and natural vegetation expansion.

WorldView-2 Mid-Scale Classification — Main Pass & South Pass

Roseau Cane Variant Separation (Delta vs. European Phragmites)

A Roseau cane habitat classification scheme was developed for the two main passes in the lower MRD using mid-scale WorldView-2 imagery. Through Support Vector Machine (SVM) classification, the two variants of Roseau cane — Delta Phragmites and European Phragmites — were successfully distinguished. The SVM results showed ~79% agreement with the Landsat-derived classification results. No significant differences were found in detected dieback areas between the two variants from 2019 to 2021.

Nine land cover classes were mapped: Bare Soil, Dead Vegetation, Delta Phragmites, European Phragmites, Man-Made, Marsh Grass, Mud, Open Water, and Shrub/Woody Vegetation. Delta Phragmites is the dominant species, accounting for 80.8% of total vegetation coverage; European Phragmites accounts for 2.7%.

04

Texture Feature Analysis (SVM + Texture Windows)

Addition of texture information only slightly improved overall classification accuracy. The 7×7 texture window achieved the highest overall accuracy (91.95%, κ = 0.91) compared to base SVM (90.80%, κ = 0.90). However, classified results showed more salt-and-pepper noise due to the fragmented appearance of Roseau cane stands in high-resolution WV imagery. Base SVM was therefore preferred for production maps.
