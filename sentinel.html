Study Overview & Motivation

Why Deep Learning on Sentinel-2?

Phragmites australis (Roseau cane) is a dominant marsh species contributing to sediment retention, shoreline stabilization, and wetland resilience in the lower MRD. Its distribution is highly dynamic, driven by fluctuations in hydrology, storm impacts, salinity intrusion, and episodic environmental stressors. Accurate and consistent annual mapping is therefore essential for monitoring ecosystem health and supporting restoration planning.

Traditional machine learning classifiers are limited in capturing spatial complexity and spectral variability of marsh vegetation. They are sensitive to seasonal changes, atmospheric conditions, and mixed pixel effects. To address these limitations, this study applied a U-Net model with a ResNet-34 encoder to multi-temporal Sentinel-2 fall composites, enabling robust pixel-level classification through an encoder-decoder architecture that integrates spatial context across years.

U-Net Model Configuration

The U-Net model (ResNet-34 backbone) was applied for pixel-level classification into 3 classes: Phragmites, Non-Phragmites, and Water. Hyperparameters — chip size, batch size, epochs, learning rate, and loss function — were iteratively tuned for stable convergence and robust generalization. Training polygons were prepared annually from 2019 to 2024 using high-resolution imagery, ensuring year-specific ground truth. Model performance was monitored through training loss, validation loss, and accuracy curves.

03

Classification Accuracy — Annual Performance (2019–2024)

Sentinel-2 fall composites (2019–2024) were used for year-wise training and validation. The model achieved high and consistent performance, with overall accuracy ranging from 90% to 98% and Kappa from 0.85 to 0.98, peaking in 2023. Phragmites class accuracy ranged from 80% to 96%, with strongest performance in 2023. Spatially, Phragmites was consistently mapped along distributary channels and marsh edges; minor errors occurred in fragmented areas.

05

Key Findings

Model Performance

U-Net (ResNet-34 backbone) produced consistently high classification accuracy (90–98%) across all six years. Accuracy declines were linked to disturbance years — not model limitations. Phragmites mapping is reliable across space, especially along distributary channels and marsh edges.

Ecological Insights

Phragmites area declined significantly during disturbance events (hurricanes, freeze, drought) and recovered rapidly afterward. The species demonstrates high sensitivity to environmental stress and strong resilience — providing a strong baseline for evaluating multi-year training approaches and longer-term change analysis.
